A variable is a named storage for data. It allows you to store and manipulate values in your code.

  • let: Block-scoped, can be updated but not re-declared within the same scope.
  • const: Block-scoped, cannot be updated or re-declared. Must be initialized during declaration.
  • var: Function-scoped, can be updated and re-declared. Hoisted to the top of its scope.

ğŸ“ Scope and Hoisting

Variables declared with let and const are block-scoped and not accessible before their declaration due to the temporal dead zone. var is function-scoped and hoisted, meaning it's accessible before its declaration but initialized with undefined.

ğŸ“ Variable Naming Rules

  • Names can include letters, digits, underscores, and dollar signs.
  • Must begin with a letter, underscore, or dollar sign.
  • Case-sensitive: myVar and myvar are different.
  • Cannot use reserved keywords like let, class, etc.
